Introduction

Education is one of the most important parts of a country's development. In this project, we provide an insight of American post-secondary education by leveraging datasets of universities and professors. Our work includes visualization of university and college information, exploratory data analysis of professors, prediction for PhD students' future workplace, etc.



Dataset Introduction

University Dataset

This dataset is published by HIFLD and consists of all Post Secondary Education facilities acknowledged by American official departments. It covers 7,686 American universities and colleges located at 50 states, as well as Puerto Rico and other assorted U.S. territories. Data Quality (4C) Evaluation

University Dataset Professor Dataset
Completeness

(a) appropriateness: The dataset contains all American university information collected in 2019. It can provide most up-to-date information for us to answer questions about universities.
(b) completeness: We acquire the dataset from opendatasoft. According to the announcement of the uploader, this dataset covers all universities acknowledged by American official departments.
(c) report errors: All the rows lacking key information or having meaningless value are eliminated. There is no typo in dataset.

The data comes from a research project at Brown university. The data is collected from voluntary voting from people from academia.

Coherence

Value of each entry is ensured to correspond to its column value type. Information in same column is recorded in same units for all universities. All universities are recorded with same kinds of information.

We specifically chose the data in the domian that we are familiar with. So that we could ensure the data make sense.
Correctness

All data are collected based on statistics. There is no subjective factors in the factual data.

All data are collected based on statistics. There is no subjective factors in the factual data.
Accountable

Definitely, we know how the data come out, what does each column in the table mean, etc. We also back up the original dataset. The detailed data processing steps are recorded in previous part.

The information of the professors are comming from voluntary voting. However, the accountability is ensured by asking the volunteer to paste the url to the professor's website. So that the information of the professor could be verified.



University Dataset Analysis

This dataset consists of all Post Secondary Education facilities acknowledged by American official departments. It covers 5897 universities and colleges located at United States, excluding Puerto Rico and other assorted U.S. territories. It only contains information of universities and colleges whose population is more than 100 and annual total enrollment is more than 0, which includes university name, state, county, school type, population, annual total enrollment, latitude and longitude.



How is the geographic distribution of universities across the United States?

The following map is designed to deliver a breif overview of school distribution across United States. From the map, we can see a highly skewed distribution where most universities and colleges with large population sit at eastern United States and west coast region. As these two regions are famous for prosperous economy and pleasant climate, we can identify that education is a high-level requirement which are pursued after the fulfillment of subsistence. Another discovery is that developed post-secondary education also needs the support of large population because the distribution of universities and colleges are correlated to the commensense distribution of American population.



What is the number of people engaging in post-secondary education in each area?

The following two graphs provide different view for readers to understand the relationship between areas and populations.
The first tree map mainly reveals the relationship between states and populations. In that graph, the states are ranked by the number of population and the counties are organized by the states it belongs to. We can see that the top three states with highest population are CA, TX and NY.


The second bubble chart presented below can provide more comparisons across cities. It reveals the relationship between counties and populations. We can find that counties with most students are Los Angeles, Maricopa and Cook.





Professor Dataset Analysis

This dataset published by Prof. Huang in Brown University. It contains information of over 2,000 computer science professors at top universitiesm, which include the names, institution, degrees obtained, and when they joined the university, for professors in the traditional role that involves both research and teaching.



Where do the professors study for their Ph.D. degree?

The schools are ranked by their ranking. As we can see from the following heat map, dark color cells are concentrated around the upper-left corner, top few rows and diagonal. That means top CS schools graduate more phds that end up in a top 50 institute, and a phd in top universities usually become a professor in a top university. The dark color in the diagonal means there is a significant portion of phd stayed in their own university as a professor.

You can interact with the following chart by putting your mouse on each cell to show the details of each cell.



To show the detailed information of the career path of the graduate within Top 10 (according to U.S. news) CS school, we plot the following chord diagram. Each connection represent the graduate transfer from one school to another school. The width at each end of the connection represent the number of graduates participating in this flow.



Which school has cultivated the most professors?
